UberDelta meets the overwhelming need for a security token exchange. Tokenized securities are the future of investment in early-stage startups, and these professional investors demand institutional grade tools and desire a single platform to manage assets on a variety of blockchain networks.

UberDelta’s advanced compliance engine enables this exchange to provide a decentralized marketplace for restricted tokens and adapt to changes in securities classifications and regulations. By working with our AML/KYC partners, we will be able to support institutional investors across the globe.

Decentralized exchanges are an elegant solution to many of the security and custody issues that centralized exchanges face by empowering professional investors to maintain custody and implement their own cybersecurity protocols. UberDelta’s goal is to become the premier regulated token exchange. We believe the demand exists and the team will execute.

UberDelta's Vision

One of the first decentralized exchange traders was UberDelta founder M. J. Dillon. When early versions of decentralized trading began to appear in 2016, he began to work closely with the founder of EtherDelta to improve that platform. Along with UberDelta team member Tim Haberham, Dillon was instrumental in helping to engage and support hundreds of the earliest decentralized exchange traders and developers.

UberDelta today is already a functional system running on the Ethereum network. Powered by one of the most advanced smart contracts ever deployed on the Ethereum network, this feature-rich, compliance-focused smart contract allows peer-to-peer leverage, the creation and trading of options on tokens, and directed trading between individual users. In addition to a number of advanced customer support features, UberDelta sports a reactive and clear user interface, and a backend order book system that prevents against fat-finger errors and eliminates the possibility of front-running directed trades.

The flexible and upgradeable compliance functionality allows restricted tokens, to trade under custom conditions. With UberDelta’s trade-level, smart contract based, compliance engine, this exchange can provide a decentralized marketplace for restricted tokens that can adapt to future changes to the international regulatory environment as they happen.

UberDelta’s vision is to evolve their existing Ethereum-based decentralized exchange into a multitude of decentralized exchanges powered by other blockchains (such as EOS and Neo) along with institutional grade position management tools and custom reporting (P&L, historical positions, tax reporting) utilizing their custom multi-chain block explorer.
